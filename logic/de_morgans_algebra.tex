\section{De Morgan's for Three in Boolean Algebra}

Use Boolean Algebra to show that the following are logically
equivalent:
\[
\neg(p \vee q \vee r) \equiv \neg p \wedge \neg q \wedge \neg r
\]

\subsection*{Proof}

\begin{align*}
\neg(p \vee q \vee r)
&\equiv \neg((p \vee q) \vee r) && \text{(Associativity)} \\
&\equiv \neg(p \vee q) \wedge \neg r && \text{(De Morgan's Law)} \\
&\equiv (\neg p \wedge \neg q) \wedge \neg r && \text{(De Morgan's Law)} \\
&\equiv \neg p \wedge \neg q \wedge \neg r && \text{(Associativity)} \qquad \square
\end{align*}
